Symptoms & Prevention

Learn about COVID-19 symptoms and how to protect yourself and others

Common Symptoms

COVID-19 affects different people in different ways. Most infected people will develop mild to moderate symptoms.

Most Common Symptoms
  • Fever
  • Dry cough
  • Fatigue
Less Common Symptoms
  • Headache
  • Loss of taste or smell
  • Sore throat
  • Runny nose
  • Muscle or joint pain
Severe Symptoms
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain or pressure
  • Loss of speech or movement
If you have severe symptoms, seek medical attention immediately!

When to Seek Medical Attention

Most people with COVID-19 recover without special treatment. However, you should seek immediate medical attention if you experience:

Emergency Warning Signs
  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Persistent pain or pressure in the chest
  • New confusion or inability to wake or stay awake
  • Bluish lips or face
Symptom Timeline

Symptoms may appear 2-14 days after exposure to the virus. The median incubation period is 5-6 days.

2-14 Days
On average, symptoms appear around 5-6 days after exposure
When to Get Tested

If you develop symptoms, have been in close contact with someone known to have COVID-19, or have attended an event where social distancing was challenging, consider getting tested.

Preventive Measures

Hand Hygiene

  • Wash hands frequently for at least 20 seconds
  • Use soap and water or alcohol-based hand sanitizer
  • Avoid touching face with unwashed hands

Mask Wearing

  • Wear a mask in public settings
  • Ensure mask covers nose and mouth
  • N95/KN95 masks offer better protection

Social Distancing

  • Maintain at least 6 feet (2 meters) distance
  • Avoid crowds and poorly ventilated spaces
  • Work from home when possible

Cleaning

  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces
  • Use appropriate disinfectants
  • Focus on high-touch areas

Ventilation

  • Improve ventilation by opening windows
  • Use air purifiers if available
  • Avoid enclosed spaces with poor airflow

Vaccination

  • Get vaccinated when eligible
  • Complete the full vaccination course
  • Get booster shots as recommended

WHO Guidelines

The World Health Organization provides comprehensive guidelines for COVID-19 prevention and management.

These guidelines are continuously updated based on the latest scientific research and global situation.

Visit WHO Website

Key WHO Recommendations

Protect yourself and others

Get vaccinated, maintain physical distance, wear a mask, and clean your hands frequently.

If you're feeling unwell

Stay home, self-isolate, and seek medical attention if you develop serious symptoms.

Break the chain of transmission

Follow local guidance, participate in testing efforts, and cooperate with contact tracing.
