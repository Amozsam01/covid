Frequently Asked Questions

The most common symptoms include fever, dry cough, and fatigue. Other symptoms can include loss of taste or smell, headache, sore throat, aches and pains, and more. Symptoms can range from mild to severe, and some people may be infected without showing any symptoms at all.

COVID-19 spreads primarily through respiratory droplets when an infected person coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes. It can also spread by touching surfaces contaminated with the virus and then touching your face. The virus can spread from people who are infected but don't have symptoms (asymptomatic) or before they develop symptoms (presymptomatic).

Yes, COVID-19 vaccines approved by regulatory authorities have undergone rigorous testing to ensure they meet safety standards. Like all vaccines, COVID-19 vaccines can cause side effects, but most are mild and temporary (e.g., pain at injection site, fatigue, headache). Serious side effects are extremely rare, and the benefits of vaccination in preventing serious illness and death from COVID-19 far outweigh the risks.

When worn correctly, masks help reduce the spread of COVID-19. They act as a barrier to prevent respiratory droplets from reaching others when the wearer coughs, sneezes, talks, or breathes. The effectiveness depends on the type of mask, how well it fits, and how consistently it's worn. N95/KN95 masks offer the highest protection, followed by surgical masks and well-made cloth masks.

If you develop symptoms of COVID-19, you should:
  1. Self-isolate immediately to avoid spreading the virus to others
  2. Contact your healthcare provider or local health department for guidance
  3. Get tested for COVID-19
  4. Monitor your symptoms and seek emergency medical care if you experience severe symptoms like difficulty breathing
  5. Follow the advice of healthcare professionals regarding treatment and isolation duration
